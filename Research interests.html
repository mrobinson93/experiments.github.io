Research interests

Short- and long-term memory architecture and processes

One research focus is on evaluating the structure and processes in visual short- and long-term memory. Currently, I pursue this line of work with Timothy Brady by developing and evaluating models of memory, primarily within a signal detection framework. I have also published on several relevant topics as a graduate student with Aaron Benjamin, David Irwin and colleagues.

Functions and models of attention

In a different line of work with David Irwin, I used behavioral methods, psychophysics and eyetracking to study some of the functions of visual attention. Much of this work shaped my current interests in visual cognition. More recently, with Mark Steyvers I examined another facet of attentional processing in a project that attempts to link computational models of two attentional control tasks.

Building rigorous psychological theories, with a specific focus on decision science

Although not my primary research area, I have an interest in fundamental issues in theory development in decision science, which I was introduced to by Michel Regenwetter. This work was also my first introduction to mathematical psychology, and I continue to have a strong interest in using proof-based analysis in my work.
