Short- and long-term memory processes and architecture

My research focuses on evaluating the structure and processes in visual short- and long-term memory. Currently, I pursue this line of work with Professor Brady by developing and evaluating models of memory, primarly within a signal detection framework. I have also published on several relevant topics as a graduate student.

Attention and perception

In a different line of work with Professor Irwin I used behavioral methods, psychophycics and eyetracking to study some of the functions of visual attention. Much of this work shaped my current interests in visual cognition and memory. More recently, I examined a different facet of attentional processing in a project that attempts to link computational models of two attentional control tasks.

Building rigorous psychological theories, with a specific focus on decision science

Although not my primary research area, I also have an interest in fundamental issues in theory development in decision science, which I was introduced to by Professor Regenwetter. This work was also my first introduction to mathematical psychology, and I continue to have a deep interest in using proof-based analysis in my work.
